Research

Summary

My work develops data-driven and physics-informed approaches to detect subtle, repeatable structure in seismic observations across laboratory and tectonic scales, with emphasis on interpretability and reproducibility.

  • Waveform similarity, clustering, and detection at scale
  • Time–frequency features and passive monitoring strategies
  • Linking signals to physical mechanisms (loading, creep, instability)

nAIpoints

What we do

nAIpoints applies time-series inference and signal processing to passive sensing problems where stress evolution, damage, or stability must be monitored without interrupting operations.

  • Earthquake hazard monitoring and precursory pattern discovery
  • Infrastructure integrity and vibration/acoustic surveillance
  • Energy and subsurface operations monitoring (where data access permits)
